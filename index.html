Project

  1. In-silico Analysis Genomic Variant of Human BDNF Gene Involved in Psychiatric Disorder

    Feb 2024 - April 2024

    Utilizing bioinformatics tools, our study delved into the impact of the V66M mutation on Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), a key player in neurogenesis and synapse formation. By analysing rs6265, rs2353512, rs4030470, rs8192466, and rs10767661 retrieved from dbsnp, SIFT and Polyphen2 were employed for functional and stability analysis. InterPro aided in identifying domain regions, while I-Mutant assessed stability changes. Phyre2 and I-Tasser facilitated structural modeling for wildtype and mutant sequences. Our findings underscore the significance of the V66M variant in schizophrenia and bipolar disorder etiology, underscoring its potential as a genetic risk factor for these conditions.

    Tools: "NCBI","UNIPROT ","PDB","Polyphen-2","I-mutant","Interpro","I-tasser", "TM align"
